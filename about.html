Who was Sachidanand

Sachidanand Kumar was born in 1929 in a small village of Bihar & dedicated his life to the pursuit and dissemination of knowledge. As a teacher, he was revered not only for his intellect but for his compassion and selflessness. He taught countless students across Bihar, many of them from underprivileged backgrounds, without charging any fee. His deep belief in the transformative power of education made him a mentor and guide to generations, inspiring them to rise beyond their circumstances.Deeply influenced by Gandhian and Nehruvian ideals, Sachidanand lived a life of simplicity, donning the humble attire of dhoti and kurta. He combined his traditional values with progressive thinking, often engaging in philosophical discussions on society, ethics, and justice. His ideas reflected a strong inclination toward Karl Marx’s thoughts on equity and upliftment of the oppressed, creating a unique blend of Indian and global philosophical currents. Though highly learned, Sachidanand never detached himself from the roots of rural India. He worked as a farmer post retirement and was closely involved with village communities, rural education, and cooperative development. His presence in the village was not just that of a thinker but of a hands-on reformer who led by example.As a freedom fighter, Sachidanand actively participated in India’s struggle for independence. Sachidanand’s life is a testament to how one individual can impact society across multiple dimensions—education, philosophy, activism, and rural development. His legacy continues to live through the students he mentored, the communities he uplifted, and the ideas he championed.

Aditya Bhardwaj, Founder
